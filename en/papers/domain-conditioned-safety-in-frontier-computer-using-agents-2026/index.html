Domain-Conditioned Safety in Frontier Computer-Using Agents: A 793-Episode Browser Benchmark, a Coding-Domain Cross-Reference, and a Reproducibility Audit of Recent Red-Teaming

arXiv 2026 65.9 benchmark

TLDR

A benchmark of 793 episodes shows frontier CUAs resist hand-crafted prompt injections on browser tasks but fail on coding tasks, revealing domain-conditioned safety.

Reasoning

The paper's strength is its large-scale benchmark and reproducibility audit, demonstrating that safety hardening is domain-specific. A weakness is the limited scope (two models, hand-crafted attacks) and lack of testing on RL-optimized injections, which may limit generalizability.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • CUA-HandCrafted benchmark: 793 episodes, 24 multi-step web tasks, 56 hand-crafted attack templates, 8 attack families, 4 system-prompt configurations
  • Reproducibility audit showing that hand-crafted prompt-injection templates fail against current frontier CUAs (0% ASR), with resistance traced to model weights via prompt ablation
  • Cross-domain comparison revealing domain-conditioned safety: browser-hardened models remain vulnerable to skill-injection in coding agents (up to 100% ASR)
  • Argument that literature's high ASR stems from RL-optimized injection text rather than attack categories, and that unreleased optimized strings make published numbers unreproducible

Methodology

The authors construct a public benchmark of 793 browser-agent episodes combining 24 multi-step web tasks with 56 hand-crafted attack templates across 8 families and 4 system-prompt configurations. They evaluate Claude Sonnet 4.6 and GPT-5.4 on multi-step attack success rate, perform a prompt ablation to localize resistance, and cross-reference results on a coding-agent benchmark (SkillBench) to test domain generalization.

Key Results

Against frontier CUAs, hand-crafted multi-step attacks achieve 0/140 success (Clopper-Pearson 95% upper bound 2.60%), with resistance residing in model weights; however, the same models fall to hand-crafted skill-injection on coding tasks at up to 100% success, demonstrating domain-conditioned safety.

Limitations

  • Only hand-crafted attack templates were tested; RL-optimized injection strings from prior work were not reproduced, so the audit does not directly measure the impact of optimized text.
  • Safety hardening is domain-conditioned and does not generalize beyond the browser surface, as shown by high vulnerability in the coding domain.
  • Benchmark is limited to 24 web tasks and one coding-domain cross-reference, potentially missing other CUA modalities or attack surfaces.

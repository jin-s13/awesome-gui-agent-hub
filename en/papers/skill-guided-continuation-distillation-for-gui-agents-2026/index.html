Reasoning

The paper addresses a key limitation of behavior cloning for GUI agents—lack of supervision on off-trajectory states—with a novel iterative self-improvement framework. Strengths include a clear problem formulation, a practical method (SGCD), and strong empirical results on a standard benchmark. Weaknesses are the single benchmark evaluation and no discussion of generalization to other domains or real-world deployment.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Skill-Guided Continuation Distillation (SGCD) iterative self-improvement framework for GUI agents
  • Generation of successful continuations from policy-induced off-trajectory states using a skill-guided policy
  • Skill extraction from both successful and failed rollouts: Continuation Plans, Critical Targets, Failure Traps, and Success Criteria

Methodology

SGCD first runs the plain policy without skill guidance for a few steps to reach realistic off-trajectory states. From these states, a skill-guided policy completes the task and produces successful continuations, which are mixed with expert trajectories to provide supervision over off-trajectory states. Skills are extracted from both successful and failed rollouts.

Key Results

On OSWorld-Verified, SGCD improves the success rate of three base models from the low-30% range to over 50%.

Uncertainty Quantification for Computer-Use Agents: A Benchmark across Vision-Language Models and GUI Grounding Datasets

arXiv 2026 70.6 benchmark

TLDR

Argus benchmarks post-hoc uncertainty quantification for GUI grounding across 4 VLM agents and 4 datasets, finding selective transfer of UQ rankings.

Reasoning

The paper's strength is its comprehensive cross-regime benchmark covering 27 open-weight and 8 closed-source methods, revealing that UQ rankings transfer across datasets but not across model classes. A limitation is its focus on single-step executable GUI grounding rather than multi-step agent tasks, and the abstract does not detail real-world deployment or user studies.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Argus benchmark: first large-scale cross-regime evaluation of post-hoc uncertainty quantification for GUI grounding agents, spanning 27 open-weight and 8 closed-source methods across multiple models and datasets.
  • Systematic analysis of UQ ranking transferability, revealing selective transfer: stable within-model across datasets but degraded across model classes and observable interfaces.
  • Introduction of conformal click regions with locally weighted disks for spatial safety, demonstrating radius shrinkage of 40–60% when plug-in UQ is calibrated.
  • Public release of per-item records, calibration/test splits, UQ scores, and analysis scripts to enable regime-aware UQ selection.

Methodology

The benchmark evaluates 27 post-hoc UQ methods (logit-based, sampling/consistency, hidden-state/density, attention-based, verbalised confidence, split-conformal) on 4 open-weight VLM agents across 4 GUI grounding datasets, plus 8 methods on 3 closed-source frontier vendors where internal states are inaccessible. Ranking stability is measured via Spearman correlation across datasets, models, and interfaces, and conformal prediction constructs locally weighted click regions to assess spatial safety.

Key Results

Within-model UQ ranking transfer across datasets is strong (Spearman ρ up to 0.969), but cross-tier transfer to closed-source models averages only +0.08, necessitating target-specific reranking. Hidden-state and density methods are the most stable open-weight family; conformal click regions shrink radii by 40–60% with calibrated UQ but suffer coverage degradation under calibration-test or interface mismatch.

Limitations

  • Benchmark is restricted to single-step executable GUI grounding, not multi-step agent trajectories.
  • Cross-model and cross-interface UQ ranking transfer is weak, so findings do not generalize across model classes without re-evaluation.
  • Conformal coverage degrades under distribution shift (calibration-test or interface mismatch), limiting deployment robustness.
  • Only post-hoc UQ methods are considered; training-based or hybrid approaches are not evaluated.

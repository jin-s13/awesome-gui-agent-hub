UI-MOPD: Multi-Platform On-Policy Distillation for Continual GUI Agent Learning

arXiv 2026 62.1 method, benchmark

TLDR

Proposes UI-MOPD, a multi-teacher on-policy distillation method for continual learning of GUI agents across platforms, achieving improved task success on OSWorld and MobileWorld.

Reasoning

Strengths: novel approach combining multi-teacher distillation with continual learning for cross-platform GUI agents, and introduction of a new dataset Uni-GUI. Weaknesses: limited to two platforms (OS and mobile) and relatively low task success rates (38.2% and 12.0%), indicating room for improvement.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Construction of Uni-GUI, a high-quality cross-platform GUI interaction dataset
  • UI-MOPD, the first method incorporating multi-teacher on-policy distillation into continual learning for GUI agents
  • Dynamic selection of platform-specific teachers and platform-conditioned distillation to transfer behavioral priors while preserving prior capabilities

Methodology

UI-MOPD incorporates multi-teacher on-policy distillation into continual learning: it dynamically selects a platform-specific teacher based on the current environment and uses platform-conditioned distillation to transfer platform-specific behavioral priors to a shared policy, enabling adaptation to new platforms while retaining capabilities on existing ones. The method is evaluated on the Uni-GUI dataset across OSWorld and MobileWorld platforms.

Key Results

UI-MOPD achieves task success rates of 38.2% on OSWorld and 12.0% on MobileWorld, demonstrating effective balancing of cross-platform capability retention and new-platform adaptation.

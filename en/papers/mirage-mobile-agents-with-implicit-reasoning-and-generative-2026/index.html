MIRAGE: Mobile Agents with Implicit Reasoning and Generative World Models

arXiv 2026 58.2 method

TLDR

MIRAGE learns continuous latent reasoning from explicit traces, enabling efficient mobile agents with world models, reducing token generation.

Reasoning

The paper introduces a novel framework that compresses explicit reasoning into latent representations and incorporates a generative world model, showing strong results on mobile benchmarks with significantly fewer tokens. However, the evaluation is limited to two mobile-specific datasets, and the approach's generalizability to other domains (e.g., web or OS agents) is not demonstrated.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Learns continuous latent reasoning representations from visible textual reasoning traces, enabling internal reasoning without decoding long rationales.
  • Incorporates a generative world-model objective that aligns latent reasoning vectors with future screenshots, encouraging anticipation of upcoming interface states.
  • Performs inference in continuous latent space, drastically reducing token generation while maintaining or improving task performance.

Methodology

MIRAGE distills explicit chain-of-thought reasoning into compact continuous latent vectors using supervised traces, and jointly trains a generative world model that predicts future screenshots from those vectors. At inference, the agent reasons implicitly in latent space and produces actions without generating long textual rationales.

Key Results

On AndroidWorld, MIRAGE matches explicit chain-of-thought fine-tuning with 3-5x fewer decoded tokens and improves an instruction-tuned baseline by 10.2 points; on AndroidControl, it improves action grounding while generating over 75% fewer tokens.

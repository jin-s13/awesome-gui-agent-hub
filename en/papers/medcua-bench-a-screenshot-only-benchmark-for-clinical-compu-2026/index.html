Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Introduces MedCUA-Bench, the first screenshot-only interactive benchmark specifically for clinical computer-use agents.
  • Covers 18 clinical scenarios across 10 medical domains, reconstructed from real product manuals and open-source medical systems to avoid licensing and privacy constraints.
  • Uses paired intent-level and step-level goals to disentangle clinical reasoning from UI execution.
  • Evaluates agents with a deterministic checker over task completion and five clinical safety dimensions.

Methodology

MedCUA-Bench is an interactive, screenshot-only benchmark comprising 18 clinical scenarios across 10 medical domains, built from real product manuals and open-source systems. Each task includes paired intent- and step-level goals, and agents are evaluated by a deterministic checker on task completion and five clinical safety dimensions. The benchmark was tested on 23 agents, including both closed-source and open-source models.

Key Results

The best closed-source agent achieves 54.2% strict success on MedCUA-Bench, while all models score below 9% on the real OpenEMR system. Open-source agents average only 2.5% success, with the best open-source model reaching 16.2%.

Limitations

  • The benchmark relies on reconstructed interfaces rather than real proprietary clinical software, which may not capture all real-world complexities.
  • The screenshot-only modality may restrict the types of interactions agents can perform compared to live environments.

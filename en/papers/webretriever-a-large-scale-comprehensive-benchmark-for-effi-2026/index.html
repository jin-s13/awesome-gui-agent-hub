Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • WebRetriever: a large-scale benchmark with 800 websites and 1,550 tasks spanning consumer, professional, and enterprise domains, capturing diverse user intent patterns
  • NavEval: a novel LLM-as-Judge framework that leverages rich interaction context beyond screenshots, achieving state-of-the-art alignment with human judgment
  • Three complementary evaluation protocols: navigation proficiency, knowledge-assisted interaction, and end-to-end task completion with information extraction

Methodology

We construct WebRetriever, a benchmark of 800 websites and 1,550 tasks across diverse domains. We propose NavEval, an LLM-as-Judge that uses interaction context beyond visual screenshots, achieving high alignment with human judgment. Agents are evaluated under three protocols: navigation, knowledge-assisted interaction, and end-to-end task completion with information extraction.

Key Results

NavEval achieves state-of-the-art alignment with human judgment on multiple evaluation datasets. Extensive experiments reveal large performance disparities across the three protocols, demonstrating that navigation success alone is an insufficient predictor of real-world effectiveness.

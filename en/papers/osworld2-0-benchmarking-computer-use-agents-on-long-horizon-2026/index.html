Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Introduction of OSWorld 2.0, a benchmark of 108 long-horizon real-world computer-use workflows with a median human completion time of ~1.6 hours.
  • Tasks designed to capture underrepresented real-world phenomena: streaming interaction, dynamic environments, cross-source reasoning, implicit-state inference, and visual-spatial precision.
  • Grounding in authentic input artifacts and realistic stateful user profile data, with separate safety reports auditing safety-sensitive execution.
  • Revealing specific failure modes of frontier agents beyond basic GUI control, such as losing track of constraints, missing mid-task information, guessing, and skipping verification.

Methodology

OSWorld 2.0 comprises 108 end-to-end computer-use tasks that require an average of 318 tool calls with Claude Opus 4.7. Agents are evaluated under a primary binary-completion metric at 500 steps, with partial scores reported. The benchmark includes safety reports and targets challenge phenomena common in real workflows but underrepresented in prior benchmarks.

Key Results

Claude Opus 4.8 with maximum thinking and batched tool calls completes 20.6% of tasks (54.8% partial score), while GPT-5.5 plateaus near 13%. Agents struggle most with constraints, mid-task information, guessing, verification, and hidden state recovery.

FraudSMSWalker: Benchmarking Agentic Large Language Models for SMS-to-Webpage Fraud Detection

arXiv 2026 66.8 method

TLDR

A benchmark for evaluating web agents' ability to detect SMS-to-webpage fraud without reputation shortcuts, revealing struggles with benign recall.

Reasoning

Strengths include a novel benchmark with URL masking and hard benign cases, addressing a gap in cross-channel fraud detection. Weaknesses are the limited scope to web agents and lack of real-world deployment evaluation; results show current agents are weak.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Introduction of FraudSMSWalker, a controlled benchmark for URL-masked SMS-to-webpage fraud judgment that suppresses reputation shortcuts (URLs, hosts, domains, IPs, redirects, reputation metadata).
  • Inclusion of hard benign cases containing login, payment, verification, or account-management elements that mimic scam flows but are legitimate.
  • Evaluation of web agents under masked browser-agent protocols with URL-visibility ablation studies.

Methodology

We construct FraudSMSWalker, a benchmark of 699 bilingual SMS-to-webpage chains (332 fraudulent, 367 benign) across ten service scenarios. Models receive only SMS context and sanitized webpage evidence, with all URL, host, domain, IP, redirect, and reputation metadata withheld. Nine web agents are evaluated under these masked protocols, and URL-visibility ablations are performed.

Key Results

Current agents detect suspicious cues but struggle to preserve benign recall and often produce positive predictions that are weakly supported by the observed webpage evidence.

Limitations

  • The benchmark artificially suppresses URL and reputation cues, which may not reflect real-world detection where such shortcuts are available.
  • The dataset is limited to 699 cases and ten service scenarios, potentially restricting generalizability.
  • Only nine web agents are evaluated, which may not capture the full range of agentic LLM behaviors.

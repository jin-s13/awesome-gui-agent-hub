Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Introduction of PPT-Eval, a benchmark of 120 PowerPoint tasks across 12 files covering content creation and editing, organized by difficulty.
  • A robust evaluation framework with task-specific rubrics that award partial credit, penalize unnecessary changes and poor aesthetics, and provide natural language feedback, achieving high correlation with human judgments (Kendall's τ-b = 0.77).

Methodology

The benchmark comprises 120 PowerPoint tasks across 12 files, covering creation and editing scenarios with difficulty levels. Evaluation uses task-specific rubrics that assign partial credit for intermediate steps, penalize unnecessary changes and poor aesthetics, and provide natural language feedback. Frontier agents like Claude-4.5-Opus are evaluated using success rate and average partial score.

Key Results

The rubric-based evaluation achieves a Kendall's τ-b correlation of 0.77 with human judgments. Frontier agents struggle, with Claude-4.5-Opus achieving only 45% success rate and 57% average partial score.

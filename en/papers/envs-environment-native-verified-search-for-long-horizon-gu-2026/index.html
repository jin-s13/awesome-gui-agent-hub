ENVS: Environment-Native Verified Search for Long-Horizon GUI Agents

arXiv 2026 58.1 method, benchmark

TLDR

ENVS uses environment-native search and filtering to train GUI agents for long-horizon tasks, outperforming online RL on OSWorld benchmarks.

Reasoning

The paper introduces a novel training-time search-and-filter pipeline that leverages live desktop environments to generate verified supervision, showing strong empirical results on OSWorld and a new noisy benchmark. However, the evaluation is limited to the OSWorld environment, and generalizability to other platforms or real-world deployments is not demonstrated.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Environment-Native Verified Search (ENVS): a training-time search-and-filter pipeline that branches over behaviorally distinct GUI actions in live VMs, verifies successful leaves, and trains from globally balanced step-level supervision.
  • OSWorld-Noisy benchmark: a dynamic benchmark for recoverable desktop interruptions that tests agents' ability to refocus, dismiss, wait, or recover under live perturbations.

Methodology

ENVS uses live OSWorld VMs to branch over behaviorally distinct GUI actions, verify successful trajectories, and then train a policy from globally balanced step-level supervision. It is compared against ARPO-style online RL on the 300-task OSWorld pool and the new OSWorld-Noisy benchmark.

Key Results

ENVS achieves 30.3 pass@8 on original OSWorld and 29.0 on OSWorld-Noisy, outperforming ARPO-style online RL while reducing compute from 184-192 to 138-153 GPU-hours. With 30% of search data, it reaches 27.0 pass@8, exceeding ARPO from the base model, and training from noisy environments preserves visual-reasoning abilities (OSWorld-G Refusal 16.7 vs 1.9, BLINK Functional Correspondence 26.2 vs 23.1).
